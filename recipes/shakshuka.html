Shakshouka



Description

Shakshouka (Arabic: شكشوكة, also spelled shakshuka or chakchouka) is a Maghrebi dish of eggs poached in a sauce of tomatoes, olive oil, peppers, onion and garlic, commonly spiced with cumin, paprika and cayenne pepper.

According to Joan Nathan, shakshouka originated in Ottoman North Africa in the mid-16th century after tomatoes were introduced to the region by Hernan Cortés as part of the Columbian exchange.

Ingredients

  • 1 onion
  • 3 tomatoes
  • 6 cloves garlic
  • 1 bell pepper
  • 1 15-oz can of chickpeas
  • 2 tbsp curry powder
  • 2 tsp oregano
  • salt, to taste
  • pepper, to taste
  • 4 eggs
  • 1 package feta cheese
  • bread, for dipping (optional)

Steps

  1. First, mince the onion and garlic. To a pan on medium heat with some oil, heat both until the onion is soft and translucent, about 4 minutes.
  2. Dice the bell pepper and add it to the pan. Cook for a few minutes until it is slightly softened.
  3. Roughly chop the tomatoes and add them to the pan. Let them cook down for about ten minutes, until the tomatoes are very soft and most of the juices are a sauce.
  4. Add chickpeas to the pan, as well as the curry, oregano, salt and pepper.
  5. Add the four eggs to the pan, and cover the pan. Let the eggs cook and then enjoy with bread and feta!
